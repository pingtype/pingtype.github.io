Article 1
These Standards are determined pursuant to Article 36, Paragraph 3 of the Air Pollution Control Act. Article 2
The terms used in these Standards are defined as follows.I. "Gasoline characteristics" mean the physical and chemical characteristics of gasoline insofar as they are able to influence the pollution emissions of gasoline engine vehicles, and includes benzene content, aromatics content, olefins content, sulfur content, oxygen content, Reid vapor pressure (RVP), and evaporation volume percentage.II. "Diesel characteristics" means the physical and chemical characteristics of diesel fuel insofar as they are able to influence the pollution emissions of diesel engine vehicles, and includes sulfur content, aromatics and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons content.III. "Benzene content" means the total amount of benzene contained in the gasoline.IV. "Sulfur content" means the total amount of sulfur and its compounds in gasoline or diesel fuel.V. "Reid vapor pressure" is a method of expressing gasoline volatility, and means the vapor pressure of gasoline at 37.8°C (100°F) when the volume ratio of vapor to liquid gasoline is four to one.VI. "Oxygen content" refers to the total oxygen weight percentage of oxygenates which have been added to gasoline. The most commonly used oxygenates are methyl tertiary-butyl ether (MTBE), ethyl tertiary-butyl ether (ETBE), tertiary-amyl methyl ether (TAME), diisopropyl ether (DIPE), ethanol, and methanol.VII. "Aromatics content" means the total amount of benzene, methylbenzene and aromatic hydrocarbon compounds above C9 in gasoline or diesel fuel.VIII. "Olefins content" means total amount of olefins in gasoline.IX. "Cetane index" is a method of expressing the ignition performance of diesel fuel, and is calculated based on the density and 50% distillation temperature in accordance with the Chinese National Standard 12016 (CNS 12016).X. "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAH) content" means the total amount of aromatic hydrocarbons contained in gasoline excluding monocyclic aromatics.XI. "Gasohol" means fossil gasoline blended with fuel ethanol, or denatured fuel ethanol not blended with fossil gasoline and used directly as a fuel.XII. "Biodiesel" means fossil diesel fuel blended with fatty acid methyl ester, or fatty acid methyl ester not blended with fossil diesel fuel and used directly as a fuel. Article 3
